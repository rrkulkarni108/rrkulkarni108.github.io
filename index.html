About

I am a second-year PhD Statistics student at Texas A&M working under the supervision of Brani Vidakovic. My research interests lie in the intersection of wavelet analysis and Bayesian statistics. I am also interested in efficient computing and machine learning in the fields of finance, medicine, and energy.

Research

I am particularly interested in the areas of Bayesian statistics, machine learning, and applied data analysis. My current research focuses on wavelet transformations for signal processing, and I am exploring methods to enhance diversity in signal representation using entropy-based techniques.
